Day 7

Great Head + Lobster Cruise

Thursday, August 6, 2026 Sleep: Bar Harbor

Timing

Time Activity
7:30 AM Breakfast — early start for trail
8:15 AM Drive to Sand Beach — Great Head trailhead
8:30 AM – 10:00 AM Great Head loop (~1.3 mi shorter / ~1.9 mi full)
10:30 AM Optional Beehive if conditions are right (see notes)
11:30 AM Return to Atlantic Oceanside — change, lunch, rest

5:00 PM Return to dock — celebrate with waterfront dinner

Parking

Sand Beach area

Sand Beach parking fills quickly — arrive before 8:30 AM or use the Island Explorer shuttle from Bar Harbor. Same lot serves Great Head and Beehive trailheads.

Safety

Cliff edges & trail closures

  • Keep kids well back from cliff edges on Great Head — exposed drops to the ocean.
  • Beehive: Only if dry, no fear of heights, 6-year-old is steady, and an adult stays behind the child on iron rungs.
  • Do NOT plan Precipice Trail — peregrine falcon closures typically run through mid-August. Check NPS alerts before the trip.

Plan

Morning

Great Head Trail

  • Start from Sand Beach parking — coastal loop with ocean views.
  • Shorter loop ~1.3 miles; full loop ~1.9 miles if everyone has energy.
  • Views of Sand Beach, the Porcupine Islands, and open Atlantic.
  • Rocky sections — good shoes and hands ready to help little ones.
Late morning

Beehive (optional — conditions permitting)

  • Iconic Acadia hike with iron rungs and cliff exposure.
  • Only attempt when: trail is dry, no one is afraid of heights, youngest hiker is steady, adult positions behind child on rungs.
  • Skip without hesitation if any doubt — Great Head alone is a win.
Afternoon

Lobster fishing & seal-watching cruise

  • Bar Harbor Whale Watch — family-friendly lobster boat experience.
  • Pull traps, learn about the fishery, watch for seals and seabirds.
  • Bring jackets — cold and windy on the water even in summer.
  • Arrive 30+ minutes early for parking and check-in.
